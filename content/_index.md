This is tanichu's (Tadahiro Taniguchi's) personal web site.

Tadahiro Taniguchi is a Professor at the [Graduate School of Informatics, Kyoto University](https://www.i.kyoto-u.ac.jp/en/). He received his Ph.D. in Engineering from Kyoto University in 2006. He began his academic career at Ritsumeikan University, serving as an Assistant Professor, Associate Professor, and Professor in the College of Information Science and Engineering before assuming his current position at Kyoto University in 2024. During his tenure at Ritsumeikan, he was also a Visiting Associate Professor at Imperial College London from September 2015 to September 2016.

Concurrently, he serves as a Senior Technical Advisor at Panasonic Holdings Corporation. He is also serving as Representative Director of the Bibliobattle Association, Director of the AI Robot Association (AIRoA), a Technical Advisor for ABEJA, Inc., and the Chair of the IEEE Cognitive and Developmental Systems Technical Committee.

His research interests include artificial intelligence, emergent systems, and cognitive and developmental robotics. He is a pioneer in the field of **Symbol Emergence in Robotics**, a constructive approach to understanding the mechanisms of language acquisition and semantic understanding from the perspective of the symbol grounding problem.

## Why "tanichu"?

tanichu is a nickname of Tadahiro Taniguchi. Tadahiro Taniguchi is written in kanji as 谷口(Taniguchi) 忠大(Tadahiro). 谷 is pronounced as "Tani," and 忠 is pronounced as "Chu" in on-yomi pronunciation in Japanese. Therefore, many people in Japan call him "tanichu."

Please call me tanichu without hesitation :-)
